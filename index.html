Hello, I'm Yufei.

I've been interested in the vividness of the living world and how we can interact with it through physical models and community knowledge making.

I grew roots for this interest from living in and getting to know people and the land in Oʻahu. I then gained the toolbox for complex systems at Minerva University where I studied computational and math modeling.

To learn more about the experiment and theory side, I've apprenticed with Prof. Mark Peifer (UNC; poster), Exa.TrkX group (FermiLab; poster), collective information processing group (Humboldt Universitat; thesis), interactive structures group (CMU; poster).

To learn more about the relationship and action side, I've stayed with Colectivo Reciclador, a community farm in Buenos Aires; Blackberry Ridge, sustainable construction in Vermont; and Lullaby Search project, an oral history and song revival team in Yunnan, China. I also tried to learn biomimicry through making a wetland flood filter and particle swarm optimization. I've also been playing with how diagrams, comics and drawing together can come into this.
